Storix ONE

Households remain the main customers of electricity companies. Storix ONE for households is carefully engineered to reduce electricity transmission losses when fulfilling residential demand, helping to cut costs for electricity provision companies.

To maximize effectiveness, we offer a one-week trial with our Storix ONE Adaption Mechanism (SOAM). With user consent, SOAM monitors electricity usage over the trial week to generate a load profile and suggest an optimal battery configuration (precise to 1kWh), which can be optimized either for one of the following:

  • Maximum energy savings
  • Maximum energy savings per dollar invested

Storix ONE would be sold to electricity providers with:

  • An upfront installation fee for us to install the device
  • A monthly charge of 30% verified electricity savings to be given to Storix in credit form. Storix can direct electricity with credits to Storix’s own business partners

Enterprise Grade
for Professional Performance

Storix ONE PRO

Storix ONE PRO is an enterprise-grade battery system that delivers professional performance. It offers a completely configurable battery size to match your load profiles and includes our thermal performance module to deliver consistently excellent performance.

Storix ONE PRO is sold with an upfront installation fee with a monthly service charge. Contact sales for pricing and deployment options.

Find Out How Storix ONE PRO Could Be Used

With enterprise grade performance, Storix ONE PRO could be used in a variety of scenarios.

Peak Shaving: Storix ONE PRO could be paired with our Storix Peak-Shaving Model. It can charge during lower-price, offpeak periods and discharge during higher-price peak periods, decreasing costs with time-variable electricity rates.

Grid & Infrastructure: Paired with the Storix Isothermal Model, it can improve grid transmission efficiency, enabling for lower energy loss in existing grid infrastructure. For future infrastructure projects, this boosted efficiency enables cheaper materials to be used during construction without sacrificing overall efficiency, helping to reduce costs of transmission wires by up to 50% in some areas.
