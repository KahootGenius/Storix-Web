How It Works

1. Intelligent Demand Modeling:
Its machine‚Äëlearning demand model begins with a general usage curve and continuously re-trains itself using real household consumption data collected through the microchip‚Äëconnected cell array. This produces a personalized and highly accurate baseline for optimization.
2. Physics-Based Optimization:
The system applies a fundamental physics principle: conductor resistance rises with temperature ($R(T) = R_0[1 + \alpha(T-T_0)]$), increasing $I^2R$ (Joule heating) losses, especially during peak‚Äëload periods. By driving the network toward a more isothermal operating condition, Storix stabilizes resistance and minimizes thermal losses.
Where do the savings come from?
By using batteries to shave peak loads, we prevent the conductors from overheating during high-demand hours. Lower peak temperatures mean lower resistance, and since power loss is proportional to the square of current ($P_{loss} = I^2R$), even small reductions in peak current and resistance yield significant energy savings.
